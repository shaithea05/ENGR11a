Printing Parts of the Prosthetic

Project 4 - Pheonix Hand

The goal of this project was to print and assemble a functional hand prosthetic. There were a lot of difficulties with printing the different parts of the hand. We used the Prusa Mini+ 3D printer and tried printing many times but had difficulties with each print. As you can see in the photo above, our initial prints did not fully print and had to be stopped midway. We believe it was due to the printer moving too fast on relatively small objects.

After multiple attempts at printing the pieces, we decided to add a 2 layer raft to the print so that there would be a stronger structure holding the print on the plate. This was a successful print.

Pictured above you can see our print with the raft going well. Most of our previous prints had not even made it this far before we had to pause / stop the print. Through a lot of trial and error, and looking at the different settings that we used along with what problems were apparent, we were able to finally print all of the pieces of our project. There is a link to the UnLimbited Pheonix Hand below to print for your own!

Assembling the Pheonix Hand

Project 4 - Pheonix Hand

Once we printed all of our pieces we assembled them all to create the hand, and then we threaded string through the hand in order to make it functional! This was a really fun process and it was interesting to see how it all worked as we were assembling it.

Starting from the top, once we had all of the pieces shown above, we set up our station. For us that meant making sure that we actually did have all of the pieces that we needed and that the supports / rafts were all removed properly (we actually relized much later that we had forgotten to remove some of the supports, luckily we were able to find a sharp object to help us remove that part without having to unassemble our whole project!)

We started by trying to follow these written instructions that were provided, but we had a tough time trying to understand which piece was what, especially when it came to the different joints. As you can see, through these instructions it is more difficult to tell that there are different sizes of fingers that you have to notice and make sure that they are assembled in the correct order. These instructions were not bad, they were just not as easy for us to understand, thus we moved to using the video tutorial (which is linked below, along with the written instructions)

We started assembling by assembling the fingers. This was a relatively easy process, and it made the rest of the project seem like it would be smooth sailing (spoiler: it was not). One of the issues we had were the prints being extremely fragile. As you can see in the image above, the side walls of the fingers where the joints connect is very frail and could tear at any moment. This made the assmebly process a little more difficult because we had to be very careful but we also had to add a lot of pressure to make sure the screws went through the areas they had to. It was also much easier to use the video tutorial for the joints because it was easy to see which screws were needed as there were many of different sizes. One thing that we did which was helpful was organizing our materials prior to starting using the previous image. This allowed us to easily find the pieces we needed when we needed them.

These were two of our easiest and best prints. The palm and gauntlet came out well on our first prints. Once we had printed the gauntlet we had to mold it to shape it cylindrically. To do this we used a heat gun in order to make the material malleable. Once we had the gauntlet heated up we used a mold provided in class to shape it. There was a slight learning curve, but once we got the hand of it, we were able to mold the shape pretty well to have a great fit.

This is an image of all of the pieces assembled together. We had not installed the strings yet to mimic the tendons, but this had the elastics and everything else. After we had all of these parts assembled, we started with the stringing. This was when we realized there was no path for the string to travel as we had not removed the organic supports inside of our prints. Luckily, we were able to properly remove them and continue strining the tendons of the hand. It was really helpful to watch the video but there was also a projection of the tendons on the board as we were working on it that was helpful to look at.

Finally, we added the rubber finger toppers and the velcro to create this functinal 3D printed prosthetic.

Reflection 4

Project 4 - Pheonix Hand

This project was really cool to make. I really liked seeing the whole process and being able to end up with a useable product by the end. I may think about pairing up with a real customer to create some sort of prosthesis for my final project because of how much I appreciated completed this project.

In addition to printing and assembling the Pheonix Hand, we created this modification of having a magnetic cup holder in the palm of the hand. This is our sketch of the modified hand idea that we started with, along with the use of it. This may contribute to an improved quality of life by allowing individuals to securly hold a few little things without having to worry about managing them with their fingers. This would also help prioritize stability of drinks and reduce the risk of spilling, specifically for newer users who may need to practice using the prosthetic hand.

Once we had our idea set, we moved onto TinkerCAD and created the design online. This is a screenshot of our modified hand computer aided design (CAD). We made sure that the cup was properly aligned with the palm of the hand to ensure stabalization. Ideally, the magnet would be inside of the filament, so it would not cause issues to the user; however, there could be problems associated with that if users are not careful because they could unintentionally connect to other metal objects. So there are definitely still some things to think about with this modification, and allow the idea to evolve as different persepctives are uncovered, but it would be an interesting modification!
