Lotus Flower Pot

Project 2 - Remix Print

The goal for this assignment was to combine two public 3D design files using CAD tools in TinkerCAD. I downloaded a flower pot file and a lotus flower file to remix them into a prettier pot. Below you will find a sketch of the print.

This sketch shows the two different print objects and how I planned to combine to create a new remixed object. Structurally, it looks difficult as the flower is hanging at an angle with many different parts to it—the petals and the overlap of them. Another thought that crossed my mind was that the center of gravity could be affected by the addition of the flower, and without support the print may fall over as it is printing.

This screenshot from TinkerCAD shows the two different objects combined together in TinkerCAD. It was difficult working with a new software, but after following the tutorials provided it was not too hard to understand. Grouping them together was difficult for me because I did not want the back of the flower to peek out inside of the flower pot, but there was no specific setting that I was able to find that would allow the objects to mesh together and erase the parts that exceeded the overlap.

Once I completed remixing the objects, I moved the new file into PrusaSlicer. The screenshot above shows the slicing setup of the Prusa Slicer for this print. For this remixed design I decided to add supports in order to account for the 3D Printer’s capabilities for overhang with the lotus flower hanging on the side of the flower pot. I added auto-generated these snug supports.

Initially looking at the print, I really liked the way it turned out. However, once it was time to clip the supports off, it became extremely difficult to tell where the support ended and the structure of the flower petal began as the print had a lot of supports that meshed with the lotus petals. As a whole, this print was very fragile as it had a thin infill, making me very cautious of trying to break the support structure filament off.

The final product had a few errors other than the supports as well. If I were to redo this print, I would definitely change the style of the supports to make it easier to take it off post print. In doing so, I would also increase the size of the lotus flower and the thickness of the petals to add to the ease of differentiating the two aspects. I would also make the flower pot thicker to allow it to be stronger and lower my weariness of breaking the print as I work to deconstruct the supporting structures. There was also a little bit of under-extrusion which could have been due to the diameter of the filament not being properly calibrated with the machine, or, the extrusion multiplier being too low.

Skater Pikachu

Project 2 - Remix Print

The goal of this assignment was to remix two different public 3D files and combine them with CAD tools in Fusion360. I decided to combine a skateboard design with a happy Pikachu design! I wanted to be able to have Pikachu properly printed on the edge of the board without having too many issues with the center of gravity or the print of the print. Below you will find a sketch of the initial idea.

This sketch shows the initial plan of having Pikachu be centered on the skateboard, but as I was combining the objects in Fusion306, I liked it much better when he was sitting on the side and wanted to try and see if it would work as a print. The main aspects of the design that could prove to be difficult (and in fact did) were the wheels of the print. This whole print is situated on four small bases that are so far apart there is a lot of room for blunders.

Using Fusion 360 was actually much more difficult for me than TinkerCAD. I had a lot of trouble grouping the two mesh objects together due to some confusion among the type of combining I was doing. Although I believe it was due to my lack of experience, I found TinkerCAD to be much easier to use. This software has a lot of functionality, and is able to perform a lot of things I believe would be useful when starting a project from scratch as a more experienced engineer.

Although not pictured, I tried to print this object three times. The screenshot above shows the slices for my initial print where I did not make any changes—which proved to be a mistake. This first print was unsuccessful as the filament was not properly sticking to the plate as it was coming out of the machine. I thought the error was due to the wheels, but when I went back to my design I noticed that the skateboard’s wheels were not properly aligned with the plate and were at a tilt instead.

Once I fixed the tilt of the object and made sure it was situated on the plane, I noticed that the skateboard wheels were very far apart and very small, making them a difficult stand for the entire print as a whole. Due to this challenge, I wanted to add some support; however, from experience with my previous design, I did not want to use the auto generated supports. For the second print, I added brim supports to the bottoms of the skateboard wheels in hopes of the skateboard staying on the plate as you can see above. Unfortunately, this print was also unsuccessful.

After talking with some of my classmates, for my third attempt at printing this design I decided to try the autogenerated support once again, but this time I changed the print settings to “Organic” style supports instead of snug supports. As illustrated above, these supports are geared more towards the overhang parts of the design.

The photo above shows the final print that I made with the organic supports. It looks a little creepy to be completely honest, but it was much easier to remove these supports than the previous ones on the Lotus Flower Pot. This print had some stringing which I have noticed is a common occurance in a lot of my prints. For my next print I hope to overcome that by changing the temperature or speed to minimize overheating the filmanet during the print. Additionally, I am not sure if this is an issue or part of the design, but the top of Pikachu along with the top of his knee were not as seamlesss as the rest of him. Overall, I enjoyed remixing objects for this project and might keep the Pokemon theme going for the rest of the design projects!

Reflection #2

Reflection on my remix projects.

This project was much more difficult than the previous one as we learned how to use two different softwares in a short amount of time. Additionally, I had a tough time deciding what I wanted to create and how I wanted it to look in the end. Once I was able to finally decide what I wanted to make, I had some trouble importing the Lotus Flower Pot files into TinkerCAD due to the size of the file and had to work around that by finding a different design. I found TinkerCAD to be easier to work with than Fusion360; however, I liked the functionality of Fusion360 more than TinkerCAD. When I worked with Fusion360 for the Skateboarding Pikachu design, it was difficult to optimize my use of the software, but with a little bit of exploring I could see that there were a LOT of useful tools within it. As a beginner, I had a great time working with both softwares and trying out different things to group together and remix two different public files, and am stoked to move onto our next project!
